Basic Terms

Noun

Noun is a word (other than a pronoun) used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things ( common noun ), or to name a particular one of these ( proper noun ).

Pronoun

Pronoun is a word that can function by itself as a noun phrase and that refers either to the participants in the discourse (e.g., I, you ) or to someone or something mentioned elsewhere in the discourse (e.g., she, it, this ).

Verb

It is referred to as any action. For example play, sing, write etc. Three forms of verbs are available.

Adverb

Adverb is the word which adds meaning to a verb. Example He plays football carefully. Carefully here is the adverb which is adding some meaning in verb.

Adjective

Adjective Add meaning to the noun. For example He is intelligent. Here intelligent is adjective.

Tenses

Tenses are used to make sentences related to different time such as past, Present and future.

Singular

When noun is one. For example Sita is singular

Plural

When noun is more than one. For example They refers to plural.
